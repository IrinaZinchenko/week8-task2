Tours in Bangkok

Bangkok is truly an amazing city. Its thousands of lights, burning both day and night, will please both adults and children. Here, tourists are greeted by all the variety of Asian exoticism: Buddhist temples, royal palaces, quaint botanical gardens and natural parks, modern technological entertainment centers.

Excursions in Russian with experienced guides, prices from $110. See the schedule for May-June 2023, choose the itinerary for walking around Bangkok and book tickets online.

Three Temples Bangkok City Tour

Bangkok is full of beautiful temples, but it’s easy to feel overwhelmed and unsure of which to visit first. This convenient city tour visits three must-see temples—Wat Traimit, Wat Pho, and Wat Arun—as well as other city sights such as markets and ethnic minority neighborhoods. This is a great tour for first-time visitors to Bangkok as you’ll learn all about the culture and religion of Thailand.

Kanchanaburi Erawan Waterfall and Elephant Care

Get out of Bangkok for the day and enjoy a full day in the Kanchanaburi area, visiting a range of natural and historic attractions. Learn about elephants at a rescue center, visit the beautiful Erawan Waterfall (and even cool off with a swim, if you like), venture into Krasae Cave, aka the Cave of the Buddha, and learn more about World War II in Thailand at the notorious Death Railway.

Reserve
Calypso Ladyboy Cabaret Show

Book your ticket for the Calypso Cabaret Show to plan an entertaining evening out in Bangkok. Talented ladyboy performers in glittering costumes treat the audience to a variety of different musical tunes accompanied by dance performances that take inspiration from Las Vegas, Korea, and Thailand. You also have the option to reserve a seat at the Calypso restaurant to enjoy a Thai meal while watching traditional Khon dance before the show.

Reserve
Erawan National Park and Bridge Over River Kwai

Learn about how World War II played out in Thailand with this day tour to Kanchanaburi, led by an English-speaking guide You'll learn about the Death Railway that was built in the early 1940s at the JEATH War Museum and pay a visit to the famed bridge that crosses over the River Kwai. You'll also pay a visit to Erawan National Park, where you'll see the 7-tiered Erawan Waterfalls.
